About Us

Seed facilities are dedicated facilities that collect, store, and protect plant seeds from various species. They employ techniques such as cryopreservation and traditional storage methods to extend seed viability. These facilities serve as repositories of genetic diversity, providing valuable resources for crop improvement and conservation efforts. They act as insurance against natural disasters and contribute to the resilience of agricultural systems. Collaboration and data sharing among seed facilities are encouraged to promote accessibility and research. Overall, seed facilities are crucial for preserving plant biodiversity and ensuring the availability of genetic resources for future generations.
